# DanceClip AI GA4 / GSC Optimization Backlog

**Prepared:** 2026-07-20
**Domain:** danceclip.org
**Decision context:** Expand the product boundary with member-only, one-generation custom driving videos while keeping public generation limited to low-risk templates.

## Data baseline

All figures below are measured from the reports supplied for this review unless marked as an inference.

| Source | Period | Measured baseline | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| GA4 | 2026-06-22 to 2026-07-19 | 49 sessions, 48 active users, 122 views, 40.82% engagement rate, 43s average engagement, 0 key events, $0 revenue | The sample is too small for conversion conclusions, but the missing conversion events make the current funnel unmeasurable. |
| GA4 device | Same period | About 79% of traffic is mobile | Mobile interaction and video payload performance are product and acquisition priorities, not polish. |
| GA4 acquisition | Same period | AI Assistant accounts for about 80% of sessions; channel and landing-page breakdowns do not reconcile cleanly to total sessions | AI referrals are already material, but attribution and session configuration need validation before trend decisions. |
| GA4 pages | Same period | `/ai-dance-generator` engages better than the homepage | The dedicated tool page should become the primary search and conversion destination; the homepage should explain the product and route intent instead of duplicating it. |
| GSC | Latest 28 days in supplied report | 9 impressions, 0 clicks, average position 40.3 | Organic visibility is at discovery stage. Index coverage and page differentiation matter more than marginal copy edits. |
| GSC queries | Same period | Impressions include `free ai dance video generator` and close variants | Search demand is explicitly utility-led and price-sensitive. The core tool page must answer “free,” input, output, limitations, and workflow clearly. |
| GSC indexing | Same period | Sitemap exposes 8 URLs; only the homepage and `/refund-policy` are indexed; 3 URLs are reported as redirects | Technical discovery/canonicalization must be resolved before scaling content. |

No prior-period export, competitor set, backlink data, Core Web Vitals field data, or AI-citation report was supplied. Those areas are **not yet evaluated**; the backlog below does not invent their values.

## Recommended execution sequence

1. **Days 0–7:** GA4 DebugView/reconciliation, sitemap and redirect export, mobile regression verification, and production feature-off check.
2. **Weeks 2–4:** Separate homepage/tool intent, deepen the generator page, establish mobile video baseline, and submit the corrected sitemap.
3. **Weeks 4–8:** Launch the first low-risk template hub pages and the safe indexable twerk demand-validation page after safety/legal review.
4. **Months 2–3:** Publish four first-party tutorials and evaluate query/page movement.
5. **Months 3–6:** Publish model benchmarks and consented case studies only after real provider evaluation data is complete.

## Reporting notes

- Use session-scoped GA4 reports when reconciling acquisition and landing pages; do not compare them directly with user-scoped totals.
- Separate **measured change** from a plausible cause. At this traffic level, a few sessions can move percentages sharply.
- Report indexing, rankings, conversions, mobile field performance, backlinks, and AI citations as **N/A / not yet evaluated** until a valid source exists.
- The next review should compare a full 28-day post-release window with the immediately preceding 28 days and annotate deployment dates.
